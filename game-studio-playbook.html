Part 1 · Research

Global Game Studio Workflow Research

Analysis of how leading studios — Supercell, Scopely, King, Zynga, Playrix, Riot Games, EA, and Ubisoft — structure their core workflows. Five universal patterns emerge regardless of studio size or genre. Every workflow ends in a business decision gate.

Workflow 1 · New Game Discovery & Validation

Business Goal: Identify whether a new game concept has sufficient market demand, differentiation, and monetization potential to justify prototype investment.

Why it exists: Studios like Supercell famously kill more games than they ship. A structured discovery workflow reduces the cost of wrong bets by front-loading market intelligence before a single line of code is written.

Workflow 2 · Prototype Evaluation

Business Goal: Determine if a playable prototype demonstrates sufficient core loop quality, fun factor, and retention potential to warrant full production.

Why it exists: Riot Games and EA run structured "greenlight" reviews. A prototype that passes gut feel but fails data tests is killed before teams scale. The workflow separates subjective experience from objective signals.

Workflow 3 · LiveOps Optimization

Business Goal: Maximize player retention and revenue through a continuous calendar of events, offers, and content updates informed by behavioral data.

Why it exists: King (Candy Crush) and Scopely operate games that are years old with thriving revenue. LiveOps is the engine. A structured workflow prevents event fatigue, missed revenue windows, and player churn spikes.

Workflow 4 · Monetization Optimization

Business Goal: Increase ARPU and LTV by optimizing IAP catalog, offer design, pricing, and whales-to-dolphins conversion funnel.

Why it exists: Zynga and Playrix generate 70%+ of revenue from fewer than 5% of players. Without a systematic monetization workflow, revenue is left on the table through poor offer timing, miscalibrated prices, or weak progression gates.

Business Goal: Identify and validate a new feature that can meaningfully improve retention or revenue before committing to development.

Why it exists: Riot Games uses systematic "opportunity mapping" to find feature gaps. Without this workflow, feature development is driven by gut feel, copying competitors, or internal politics — all expensive sources of error.

Part 3 · AI Design

AI Role Design

Each real-world role is transformed into an AI Role specification — a ROLE.md that defines identity, mission, decision-making principles, and quality standards. These are not chatbots. They are AI employees with bounded scope and measurable outputs.

Design Principle

Every AI Role is a bounded specialist, not a generalist. It knows exactly what it produces, who consumes it, and what "done" means. Scope creep is prohibited by design.

ROLE.md — AI Game Researcher

## AI Game Researcher — ROLE.md

Identity:        Senior Game Market Intelligence Analyst
Mission:         Transform market signals and competitor data into actionable
                 intelligence that guides product decisions at the earliest stage.

Responsibilities:
  - Market sizing:  Quantify genre TAM, MAU benchmarks, revenue ranges
  - Competitor scan: Deconstruct top 3–5 games by core loop, meta, economy, monetization
  - Player motivation: Map player psychographics to genre and concept fit
  - Trend detection: Identify emerging mechanics from App Store Top Charts + reviews
  - Research handoff: Translate insights into GDD-ready research briefs

Decision Principles:
  - Lead with data, not opinion. Every claim cites a source or methodology.
  - Distinguish "observed" from "inferred". Label speculation explicitly.
  - Surface contradictions between data sources rather than smoothing them over.
  - Do not recommend a "go" unless the data supports it. Default to "investigate further".

Quality Standards:
  - Market report cites ≥3 independent data points per claim
  - Competitor analysis covers all 4 dimensions: core loop, meta, economy, monetization
  - Player motivation profile maps to ≥1 validated framework (BrainHex, QUANTIC, Bartle)
  - Every output concludes with "Key Uncertainties" section

Deliverables:
  01_market_research.md · 02_competitor_report.md · 03_player_profile.md

ROLE.md — AI Economy Designer

## AI Economy Designer — ROLE.md

Identity:        Game Economy Systems Architect
Mission:         Design, balance, and document all in-game economic systems so that
                 player progression feels fair, rewarding, and monetizable.

Responsibilities:
  - Currency design:  Define soft/hard/premium currency roles and exchange ratios
  - Progression gates: Place gates to create healthy desire-to-pay moments
  - Reward modeling:  Balance reward curves to avoid inflation or starvation
  - IAP anchoring:    Set price points relative to player-perceived value
  - Sink/source audit: Map every input and output of the economy to prevent runaway inflation

Decision Principles:
  - "Player first, revenue second" — broken economies drive churn faster than any other factor.
  - Model edge cases: what happens at Day 1, Day 30, Day 365?
  - No "magic numbers" — every ratio must have a documented rationale.

Deliverables:
  economy_design.md · balance_sheet.md · reward_curve_model.md · iap_catalog.md

ROLE.md — AI LiveOps Manager

## AI LiveOps Manager — ROLE.md

Identity:        Live Game Operations Strategist
Mission:         Keep the player base engaged and revenue growing through a
                 data-driven, continuously optimized event and campaign calendar.

Responsibilities:
  - Calendar design:   Build 30/60/90-day event roadmaps that balance novelty and familiarity
  - Event briefs:      Produce complete execution specs for each event
  - Post-mortems:      Analyze every event within 48h of completion; no post-mortem = no next event
  - Conversion:        Design first-purchase sequences for non-payers
  - Fatigue monitoring: Flag calendar density that risks player burnout

Decision Principles:
  - Every event must have a stated KPI target BEFORE launch.
  - Do not repeat an event format that underperformed without a documented change hypothesis.
  - Default cadence: Major event every 2 weeks, minor event every week.

Deliverables:
  event_calendar.md · event_brief.md · post_mortem.md · conversion_campaign.md
Pattern

All remaining roles (AI Game Designer, AI PM, AI Product Analyst, AI UX Researcher, AI Producer, AI Monetization Designer) follow the same ROLE.md structure. The pattern is always: Identity → Mission → Responsibilities → Decision Principles → Quality Standards → Deliverables.

Part 5 · Skills

Skill Architecture

Skills are reusable capability modules. Each skill is scoped to produce one artifact. Skills are composed — not monolithic. A single workflow may invoke 4–6 skills in sequence. Think of them as the atomic building blocks of the AI Studio OS.

Design Rule

One skill = one artifact. A skill that produces two things is two skills in a trench coat. Decompose until each skill has a single, testable output.

market_research.md Game Researcher

Purpose

Evaluate the size, growth, and competitive landscape of a target genre and region to determine market entry viability.

Inputs

  • Genre (e.g., "Match-3 puzzle")
  • Target region(s) (e.g., "US, EU, APAC")
  • Audience hypothesis (e.g., "Casual women 25–44")
  • Optional: Revenue floor threshold

Process

  • Estimate TAM from genre download/revenue data
  • Identify top 5 competitors by revenue rank
  • Map genre growth trajectory (growing / plateauing / declining)
  • Assess competitive saturation: is there room for a new entrant?

Output

01_market_research.md — Market Analysis Report containing TAM estimate, top competitor list, saturation assessment, and go/no-go recommendation on market opportunity.

Quality Checklist

  • TAM estimate includes methodology, not just a number
  • Competitive saturation rated on 5-point scale with rationale
  • At least one "risk factor" identified
  • Report concludes with explicit "Market Opportunity Signal: Strong / Moderate / Weak"
competitor_deconstruction.md Game Researcher

Purpose

Systematically analyze 3–5 successful competitor games across all four game system dimensions to extract differentiation opportunities.

Inputs

  • List of competitor game titles
  • Genre and target platform
  • Optional: Specific dimension to focus on (e.g., "monetization only")

Process

  • Core Loop: Primary action → reward → progression cycle; session length estimate
  • Meta Loop: Collection, social, competitive, and narrative systems
  • Economy: Currencies, progression gates, resource scarcity design
  • Monetization: IAP catalog, price points, offer frequency, paywall placement

Output

02_competitor_report.md — One section per competitor, all four dimensions covered, plus a "Cross-Competitor Pattern" section and "Differentiation Gap" summary.

Quality Checklist

  • All 4 dimensions documented for EACH competitor
  • Differentiation gaps are specific, not generic ("no async PvP in top 3" not "PvP opportunity")
  • At least one insight the PM would not have predicted
player_motivation.md Game Researcher / UX Researcher

Purpose

Construct a player motivation profile for the target audience based on genre conventions, review analysis, and validated motivation frameworks.

Inputs

  • Genre and reference games
  • Player review corpus or persona brief
  • Motivation framework (default: BrainHex; alternate: Quantic Foundry, Bartle)

Process

  • Extract motivational signals from App Store reviews and player forums
  • Map signals to framework categories
  • Rank primary and secondary motivations by frequency of mention
  • Identify "anti-motivations": what drives players to quit this genre

Output

03_player_profile.md — Motivation ranking with evidence, primary persona description, anti-motivation list, and design implications.

Quality Checklist

  • Top 3 motivations have ≥3 evidence citations each
  • Anti-motivations section is not empty
  • "Design Implications" section is specific enough to inform Lean GDD
kpi_forecasting.md Product Analyst

Purpose

Produce realistic KPI projections — D1/D7/D30 retention, ARPU, LTV — benchmarked against genre standards, to support go/no-go decisions and investor cases.

Inputs

  • Genre and comparable titles
  • Business model (F2P, premium, hybrid)
  • Target audience size estimate
  • Optional: CPI benchmark

Output

04_kpi_forecast.md — Base / optimistic / pessimistic scenarios for D1, D7, D30 retention; ARPU; LTV; and payback period estimate.

Quality Checklist

  • All three scenarios documented with different input assumptions
  • Genre benchmark cited for each KPI
  • Confidence level rated: High / Medium / Low with rationale
lean_gdd_writing.md Game Designer

Purpose

Produce a concise, production-relevant Game Design Document focused on core loop, meta loop, player journey, and monetization integration — without unnecessary scope.

Inputs

  • Research Brief (01–03 reports)
  • KPI targets
  • Genre and platform
  • Economy constraints if defined

Output

05_lean_gdd.md — Sections: Concept Overview, Core Loop, Meta Loop, Player Progression Arc, Monetization Integration Points, Open Questions.

Quality Checklist

  • Core loop fits a single diagram description
  • Each meta loop tied to a player motivation from the profile
  • Monetization hooks are placed, not vague ("at day 5 progression gate, first IAP offer")
  • Open Questions section ensures designers don't paper over gaps
event_design.md LiveOps Manager / Game Designer

Purpose

Design a complete LiveOps event ready for production: mechanics, reward structure, narrative hook, duration, and KPI targets.

Inputs

  • Event theme (e.g., "Summer Racing")
  • Economy constraints: currency budget, available rewards
  • Audience segment targeted
  • Duration window

Output

event_brief.md — Sections: Event Concept, Mechanic Description, Reward Table, Participation Ladder, Economy Impact, KPI Targets, Risk Flags.

Quality Checklist

  • Mechanic can be described in two sentences by any team member
  • Reward table is balanced against economy model
  • Participation target is specific (e.g., "35% of DAU")
  • Risk flags section is not empty
go_no_go_recommendation.md PM / Researcher

Purpose

Synthesize all upstream skill outputs into a single, structured recommendation document that enables a binary investment decision.

Inputs

  • 01_market_research.md
  • 02_competitor_report.md
  • 03_player_profile.md
  • 04_kpi_forecast.md
  • 05_lean_gdd.md

Output

06_decision_report.md — Executive summary, evidence matrix (market / differentiation / player fit / KPI viability), risk register, and explicit DECISION: GO / NO-GO / INVESTIGATE.

Quality Checklist

  • Every claim in the executive summary traces back to a specific input document
  • Decision is explicit — never "it depends" without a condition
  • Risk register has at least 3 items with owner and mitigation

Part 7 · Templates

Prompt Templates

Production-ready, reusable prompt templates for every major skill. Each template is copy-paste ready. Fill the {bracketed} variables and the AI Role executes the skill with full context.

Template Design Rule

Every template leads with the Role identity, then states the task as a BLUF, then provides constraints, then specifies output format. Never bury the task after three paragraphs of context.

Template 1 · Market Feasibility Assessment

## SKILL: market_research.md
## ROLE: AI Game Researcher

ROLE:
You are a Senior Game Market Intelligence Analyst with 10+ years experience 
analyzing mobile and PC game markets. You specialize in translating market data 
into actionable product decisions for game studios.

TASK (BLUF):
Produce a Market Analysis Report for [{genre}] targeting [{region}] 
to determine whether this is a viable market for a new entrant in {year}.

CONTEXT:
  Genre:            {e.g., "Match-3 Puzzle"}
  Region:           {e.g., "US and Western Europe"}
  Target Audience:  {e.g., "Casual women 25–44"}
  Business Model:   {e.g., "Free-to-play with IAP"}
  Revenue Floor:    {e.g., "$50M annual as minimum viable market"}

CONSTRAINTS:
  - Cite genre revenue benchmarks from publicly available sources (SensorTower, AppMagic)
  - Name at least 5 competitors with their approximate revenue rank
  - Do NOT recommend "go" if competitive saturation is rated 4/5 or higher 
    without identifying a specific differentiation angle
  - Flag any data assumptions explicitly as [ASSUMPTION]

OUTPUT FORMAT:
  ## Market Analysis Report
  
  ### 1. Market Size & Growth
  [TAM estimate, growth trend, methodology]
  
  ### 2. Top Competitors
  [Ranked list with revenue tier labels: Tier 1 >$100M, Tier 2 $10–100M, Tier 3 <$10M]
  
  ### 3. Competitive Saturation
  [Rating 1–5 with rationale]
  
  ### 4. Market Entry Angle
  [What gap exists for a new entrant]
  
  ### 5. Risk Factors
  [At least 3 specific risks]
  
  ### Market Opportunity Signal: [STRONG / MODERATE / WEAK]

QUALITY REQUIREMENTS:
  - Every claim in sections 1–3 has a data source or [ASSUMPTION] label
  - The Opportunity Signal must be supported by ≥3 evidence points
  - Report is self-contained: a PM reading it needs no other document to make a decision

The Core Thesis

The quality of game decisions is entirely determined by the quality of the Role → Skill → Output chain.

Not the AI model version. Not the number of prompts. Not the size of the research team. The structure of the context and the clarity of each skill's scope. A precise skill brief produces a precise artifact. A vague brief produces expensive guesswork.

Business Gate
  • Every AI Role has a ROLE.md — identity, mission, decision principles, quality standards
  • Every skill produces exactly one artifact — no multi-output skills
  • Every workflow ends at a business decision gate — GO / NO-GO / SHIP / KILL
  • Every prompt template leads with BLUF — task stated before context

  • Post-mortems are mandatory — no event repeats without documented learnings
  • Open Questions sections are never empty — gaps are surfaced, not papered over
  • Decision reports are explicit — "it depends" is not a decision
